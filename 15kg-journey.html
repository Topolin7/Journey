Breakfast · 1
Quark Berry Bowl
High protein, keeps you full until lunch. Frozen berries work perfectly — no need for fresh.
Ingredients (serves 2)500g low-fat quark · 200g frozen mixed berries · 4 tbsp oats · 2 tsp honey · handful of walnuts
How to make it
  1. Divide quark into 2 bowls.
  2. Thaw berries (microwave 2 min or leave in fridge overnight).
  3. Top with berries, oats, honey and walnuts. Done in 3 minutes.
Breakfast · 2
Scrambled Eggs on Rye
Classic, quick, satisfying. Rye bread keeps blood sugar steadier than white bread.
Ingredients (serves 2)4 eggs · 1 tbsp butter · salt & pepper · 4 slices dark rye bread · 2 tomatoes (sliced)
How to make it
  1. Whisk eggs with salt and pepper.
  2. Melt butter in a pan on low heat, add eggs, stir gently until just set.
  3. Serve on rye bread with sliced tomatoes on the side.
Breakfast · 3
Overnight Oats with Apple
Prep the night before — grab and go in the morning. Zero effort when you wake up.
Ingredients (serves 2)160g rolled oats · 400ml milk (or oat milk) · 2 apples (grated) · 1 tsp cinnamon · 2 tbsp chia seeds · honey to taste
How to make it
  1. Mix all ingredients in a jar or bowl the evening before.
  2. Stir well, cover and refrigerate overnight.
  3. In the morning eat cold, top with a little extra honey if you like.
Breakfast · 4
Greek Yoghurt Parfait
Feels indulgent. Actually very nutritious. Takes under 3 minutes to assemble.
Ingredients (serves 2)500g full-fat Greek yoghurt · 1 banana (sliced) · 4 tbsp granola · 2 tbsp peanut butter · drizzle of honey
How to make it
  1. Divide yoghurt into 2 bowls or glasses.
  2. Top each with banana, granola, peanut butter and honey.
Breakfast · 5
Avocado Toast with Egg
Healthy fats and protein together = long-lasting energy all morning.
Ingredients (serves 2)2 ripe avocados · 4 slices wholegrain bread · 2 eggs · squeeze of lemon · salt · chilli flakes (optional)
How to make it
  1. Toast the bread. Mash avocado with lemon juice and a pinch of salt.
  2. Fry or poach the eggs to your liking.
  3. Spread avocado on toast, top with egg and chilli flakes.
Breakfast · 6
Banana Oat Pancakes
Just 2 main ingredients, no flour needed. Naturally sweet — kids love them too.
Ingredients (serves 2)2 ripe bananas · 4 eggs · pinch of cinnamon · butter or coconut oil for frying · berries to serve
How to make it
  1. Mash bananas well in a bowl. Whisk in eggs and cinnamon.
  2. Heat butter in a pan, pour small rounds of batter.
  3. Cook 2 min each side on medium heat. Serve with berries.
Breakfast · 7
Cottage Cheese Toast
Hüttenkäse is very high in protein and very low in fat. Surprisingly filling for so little effort.
Ingredients (serves 2)250g cottage cheese · 4 rye crispbreads (Knäckebrot) · 1 cucumber (sliced) · radishes · black pepper · fresh dill
How to make it
  1. Spread cottage cheese generously on each crispbread.
  2. Top with cucumber slices, radishes, pepper and dill. Done.

One pan, 30 minutes, done. Serve with rice or a slice of bread.

Lunch · 4
Sweet Potato & Chickpea Bowl
Hearty, filling and plant-based. Make double and eat it again tomorrow.
Ingredients (serves 2)2 sweet potatoes · 1 can chickpeas · 2 handfuls spinach · 2 tbsp tahini · lemon · garlic · olive oil · smoked paprika
How to make it
  1. Cube sweet potatoes, roast at 200°C for 25 min with oil and paprika.
  2. Drain chickpeas, fry in a dry pan until crispy, about 5 min.
  3. Serve over fresh spinach, drizzle with tahini mixed with lemon juice.

Lunch · 6
Turkey & Vegetable Soup
Warm, low in calories, very comforting. Make a big pot — freezes perfectly.
Ingredients (serves 4)300g turkey mince · 2 carrots · 2 celery stalks · 1 onion · 1 can tomatoes · 1.5L chicken stock · thyme · salt
How to make it
  1. Brown the turkey mince in a large pot, breaking it up.
  2. Add onion, carrot, celery — cook 5 min stirring.
  3. Pour in tomatoes and stock, add thyme. Simmer 20 min. Season well.

Loaded Veggie Omelette
Eggs are not just for breakfast. A big veggie omelette is the perfect fast lunch.
Ingredients (serves 2)4 eggs · 1 bell pepper · 1 small onion · 100g mushrooms · 50g feta cheese · butter · salt & pepper
How to make it
  1. Whisk eggs with salt and pepper.

  3. Pour eggs over veg on low heat. Once set, crumble feta on top, fold and serve.

Tofu & Vegetable Stir-Fry
Plant-based, very high in protein, ready in 20 minutes. Serve with brown rice.
Ingredients (serves 2)400g firm tofu · 1 carrot · 1 bell pepper · 1 pak choi or cabbage · soy sauce · sesame oil · 2 garlic cloves · ginger · brown rice
How to make it
  1. Press and cube tofu. Fry in a little oil until golden on all sides, about 8 min.

  3. Add soy sauce and sesame oil. Serve over brown rice.

Ready in 20 minutes. No meat needed — beans give all the protein you need.
Ingredients (serves 2)2 cans white beans · 200g fresh spinach · 1 can tomatoes · 1 onion · 3 garlic cloves · 100ml cream · olive oil · fresh thyme
How to make it
  1. Fry onion and garlic in olive oil 4 min until soft.

  3. Stir in spinach and cream. Season well. Serve with crusty bread.

Cod with Lemon & Herb Potatoes
Light white fish, low in fat. Kabeljau is available in every German supermarket.
Ingredients (serves 2)2 cod fillets · 500g baby potatoes (halved) · 2 tbsp olive oil · 1 lemon · fresh parsley · 2 garlic cloves · salt · pepper

  3. Serve together with lemon wedges on the side.

01
The Plate Method — zero counting needed
½ your plate = vegetables · ¼ = protein (meat, fish, eggs, legumes) · ¼ = carbs (potato, rice, bread). Follow this at every meal and your portions naturally balance themselves. No apps, no scales needed.
02
Water is your secret weapon
Drink a full glass of water before every meal. Research shows this significantly reduces how much you eat. Target 2 litres per day — herbal tea counts. Your brain often confuses thirst with hunger.
03
Sunday Meal Prep = weekday success
Every Sunday, cook 2 big dishes (e.g. lentil soup + chicken pan). Portion into containers. When you're tired on Wednesday and don't want to cook, you have healthy food ready. This single habit prevents most bad eating decisions.
04
Your fridge is your diet plan
What isn't in the house can't be eaten. Always keep stocked: quark, eggs, Greek yoghurt, frozen vegetables, canned lentils, oats, nuts, and fruit. No junk in the trolley = no junk in the body. Simple.
05
Eat slowly — your brain needs 20 minutes
Fullness signals take 15–20 min to reach your brain from your stomach. Put your fork down between bites. No screens at mealtimes. You will naturally eat less without even trying to.
06
Sleep is nutrition — not optional
Sleeping under 7 hours raises ghrelin (hunger hormone) by up to 24% and lowers leptin (fullness hormone). You will feel hungrier and crave sugary food all day. Getting 7–8 hours sleep is as important as what you eat.
07
Protein at every meal = less hunger all day
Protein is the most filling nutrient. Start your day with it (eggs, quark, yoghurt) and include it at every meal. Within 2 weeks you will notice you stop craving snacks between meals.
08
The 80/20 rule — guilt-free flexibility
Eat well 80% of the time. The other 20% — a piece of cake, a glass of wine, pizza night — is completely normal and healthy. Strict restriction creates obsession and bingeing. Flexibility creates a sustainable lifestyle.
09
Never arrive starving at a meal
When very hungry, you eat fast and far more than you need before fullness kicks in. Have a small planned snack 1–2 hours before main meals. An apple or a small yoghurt is enough to take the edge off.
10
Weigh yourself weekly, not daily
Daily weight swings 1–2kg from water retention, salt, digestion — it means nothing and kills motivation. Weigh once a week, same day, same time, morning after using the toilet. That number tells the real story.
